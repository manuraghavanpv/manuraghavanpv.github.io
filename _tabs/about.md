I'm a PNRR PhD fellow working on the translation of advanced diffusion MRI methods on 3T Siemens scanners.

Using double diffusion encoding, Correlation Tensor MRI (CTI) helps us to probe brain tissues with unparalleled specificity without biophysical assumptions about the underlying anatomy. My PhD focuses on developing a fast and reproducible human CTI sequence sensitive to the microscopic changes like dendritic beading and neuronal swelling observed in the "silent" stages of dementia and stroke. Further, my research also aims to develop a scanner-level denoising strategy for Siemens Open Recon customers.
