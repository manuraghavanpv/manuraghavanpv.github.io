**I'm a PNRR PhD fellow working on the translation of advanced diffusion MRI methods on 3T Siemens scanners.**

Supervised by [Prof. Jorge Jovicich](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=lWg8Z2oAAAAJ&hl=en), my research focuses on implementing [Correlation Tensor MRI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2020.116605) on clinical 3T Prisma scanners to disentangle the biophysical origins of diffusional kurtosis in the human brain. Using double diffusion encoding, CTI helps us to probe brain tissues with unparalleled specificity with novel contrasts such as microscopic kurtosis (μK) sensitive to the cell-level changes like dendritic beading and neuronal swelling observed in the "silent" stages of dementia and stroke. Further, my research also aims to develop a scanner-level denoising strategy for Siemens OpenRecon customers.
